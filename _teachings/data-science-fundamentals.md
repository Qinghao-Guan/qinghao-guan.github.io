## Learning outcome
By the end of the seminar, students will be able to critically engage with and apply fundamental NLP techniques to analyze social media data in the context of communication research. They will understand how textual data from platforms such as Instagram, YouTube, and/or Reddit can be collected ethically, cleaned systematically, and explored using frequency-based methods. Students will be able to conduct sentiment analysis with different approaches, including rule-based lexicons, supervised classifiers, and pretrained transformer models. They will learn how to implement and interpret topic modeling methods like LDA and BERTopic to uncover dominant themes in large-scale online discourse. Also students will know about the state-of-the-art GenAI methods for their analysis of textual data. Furthermore, students will develop the skills to design their own small-scale NLP studies, formulating research questions aligned with communication theory, selecting appropriate data sources, and justifying methodological choices. Lastly, the seminar will foster a critical awareness of algorithmic bias and ethical research practices in social media research using NLP methods, preparing students to be both skilled and responsible analysts of digital communication.
